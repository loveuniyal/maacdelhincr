Top VFX Industry Trends to Watch: Complete VFX Career Guide

June 10, 2026 MAAC Delhi NCR 5 min read
Visual Effects (VFX) has transcended beyond simple green screen overlays. With the integration of real-time render engines and virtual cameras, the line between production and post-production is blurring. What are the key trends you should watch to stay ahead?

The Rise of Virtual Production & Real-time Rendering

Historically, VFX assets were added months after shooting live action. Today, LED volume stages powered by Unreal Engine allow filmmakers to project photorealistic 3D backdrops behind actors in real-time. This camera tracking system synchronizes lighting and perspectives on set, drastically cutting down green screen rotoscopy workloads.

Core Software Mastered in VFX Courses

Professional VFX artists master specialized software suites to isolate green screen elements, composite images, and simulate physical elements. The curriculum at MAAC Delhi NCR focuses on these major tools:

Foundry Nuke
The absolute standard for node-based compositing, multi-pass rendering, and clean plate integration in cinema.
SideFX Houdini
The industry standard for node-based procedural physics simulations, fluids, smoke, fire, and destruction effects.
Boris FX Mocha
Planar tracking software used to trace camera movements and apply graphic textures onto moving surfaces.
Adobe After Effects
Layer-based tracking and graphics software widely used for motion graphics, title sequences, and commercial VFX.

VFX Roles That Are In High Demand

  • Compositing Artist: The final stage. Merge CG assets, physical elements, matte paintings, and real footage seamlessly.
  • FX Technical Director: Focus on natural physics simulations—creating storms, oceans, building collapses, and magical blasts.
  • Roto & Paint Artist: Detail-oriented roles isolating characters from backgrounds and cleaning up wires or unwanted objects.
  • Matchmove Artist: Replicate real camera movement in a virtual environment to align live footage with CG components.
